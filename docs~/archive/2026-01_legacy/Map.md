
# Star Map System - A Long Way to the Top

## Overview

The **Star Map** in *A Long Way to the Top* defines the journey of your Band through interconnected planetary systems, combining **strategic traversal**, **emergent narrative**, and **roguelike deckbuilder combat**. Players move from node to node, choosing paths with varying levels of risk and reward, balancing preparation, cohesion, and ambition.

---

## Node Types

### Gig (Planet, Asteroid, etc)
- **Combat node** where the Band performs some of their Songs.
- Each Gig affects **Fan growth**, **Musician Stress**, and **Card Rewards**.
- Each Gig has an associated difficulty level, possible modifiers, and crowd archetypes.

### Boss Gig
- A multi-phase elite Gig with intense audience expectations and higher complexity.
- Ends a **Sector** or marks a major story milestone.
- Provides legendary rewards (rare cards, unique Song upgrades, special traits).

### Rehearsal Day (Spaceship Node)
- Occurs between Gigs.
- The Band spends the day aboard their spaceship, choosing **one** of the following actions:
  - **Compose** a new song (via the Modular Composition Minigame).
  - **Practice** songs to restore their "Rehearsed" status.
  - **Band Talk** to resolve conflicts.
  - **Chill** to reduce Stress across the Band.

**Conflicts left unresolved will lower Cohesion after each node**, similar to a poison effect. If **Band Cohesion** drops to 0 → the Band breaks up, **Game Over**.

### Story Event
- Optional, narrative-rich encounters with decisions and consequences.
- Can trigger new Songs, Bandmate insights, random rewards, fan loss/gain, or Empire choices.

---

## Pathing System

- **Player moves node-by-node** through a procedurally generated star system.
- Some routes are longer (more rehearsal), some are shorter (quicker gigs).
- Players can see which type of node lies ahead.
- **Some Gigs are marked with a warning icon** indicating **Empire Activity** (high risk, high reward).
- **The player cannot backtrack**.

---

## The Empire (Narrative Layer)

The **Empire** is a hegemonic power controlling many planets. Players can:

- **Oppose the Empire**: Performing subversive music, triggering narrative consequences and powerful but risky Gigs.
- **Support the Empire**: Gain safety and resources at the cost of integrity, altering Band perception and potential endings.
- **Ignore the Empire**: Focus purely on survival and fame, but face random consequences.

### Empire Activity Warning

- Appears over certain **Nodes** on the map.
- Signals surveillance, censorship, or audience risk.
- Higher **rewards** and more **Vibe-sensitive enemies**.

---

## Strategic Tension

Each path is a trade-off:
- More nodes = more rehearsal, less risk, better cohesion.
- Shorter routes = faster gigs, better rewards, higher Stress and conflict risk.
- Ignoring a conflict or song can spiral into a Band Breakdown or failed performance.

---

## Summary

The Star Map ties together:
- Card economy
- Song generation
- Musician stress/conflict
- Narrative branching
- Difficulty progression

It gives structure to the journey while letting players tell their own story of struggle, stardom, and rebellion.
