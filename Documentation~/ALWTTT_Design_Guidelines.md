# ALWTTT Design Guidelines (Inspired by Hearthstone, Monster Train, and More)

This document compiles key design lessons and inspirations from your handwritten notes, categorized into thematic areas with tiered prioritization to guide development.

---

## 🔝 Tier 1 — Must-Haves

### 🎯 Simplify, But Keep It Deep
- Design **simple cards** with **emergent interactions**.
- Avoid over-complication; strive for clarity in effects.
- Let depth emerge through **synergies**, **positioning**, and **timing**.

**Example:** A "Solo" card that simply boosts Vibe if the musician is in front — but has amplified effect if followed by a “Backup Harmony” card played by another member.

---

### 🎵 Cards Should Have Identity
- Each card should evoke a **performance** and **emotion** — not just a mechanic.
- Use **genre-specific flavor** and themes (e.g., Funk = groove, Metal = rage).

**Example:** “Groove Loop” (Funk): boosts Emotion and resets combo timer.  
“Virtuous Solo” (Classical): gains bonus Vibe if no other musician acted this turn.

---

### ⏱ Immediate Fun
- Ensure the **first gig** and **first turn** are exciting and intuitive.
- Feedback should be immediate: lights, animations, crowd reactions.

**Example:** The first card played triggers spotlights, sound effects, and fan cheers.

---

### 🔁 Broader Strategy
- Let players develop **long-term plans**:
  - Upgrade cards
  - Remove weak ones
  - Manage stress/resources
  - Choose between safe/chaotic concert paths

**Example:** Accepting a “No Rehearsal” node gives high-reward cards but raises initial stress.

---

## ⭐ Tier 2 — Highly Valuable

### 💡 Support Player Stories
- Use **random elements** (fans, events, conflict cards) to generate drama.
- Reinforce narrative arcs across a run (like the arc of a band’s rise/fall).

**Example:** A “Rival Band” shows up multiple times across a tour, evolving based on your choices.

---

### 🎛 Stylish, Responsive UI
- Menus must be fast, legible, and beautiful.
- Use **visual shorthand** (icons, genre colors) for fast play.

**Example:** Cards glow when they synergize with the current Audience Mood.

---

### 🎭 Disguise the Grind
- Add **little victories** and **micro-surprises** to reduce repetition.
- Include collectibles, rare fans, rotating events.

**Example:** After 3 concerts, a weird fan appears and offers a powerful one-time card — or a mysterious curse.

---

## 🔬 Tier 3 — Bonus Depth

### 🎚 Point Allocation System
- Allow musicians to gain **skills or traits** slowly over time.
- Consider **perks**, **passives**, or **signature moves** tied to instruments.

**Example:** Guitarist gets “Improvisation Boost” — gaining +1 Emotion when playing random cards.

---

### 💖 Emotional Design Matters
- Ensure the game has **pacing**: intensity, relief, triumph.
- Use visuals, music, and narrative for emotional arcs.

**Example:** After a tense conflict, the band plays a gig in total sync — triggering glowing harmony effects and a cathartic soundtrack.

---

### 🧬 New Game Plus / Long-Term Systems
- Plan for multiple runs with **increased difficulty** or **band legacy systems**.
- Could unlock **new genres**, **fan types**, or **tour formats**.

**Example:** After winning a campaign, you unlock “The Underground Scene” — a gritty tour with experimental fans and cards.

---

## 📚 Bonus — Design Philosophy Notes

### From Hearthstone (Eric Dodds)
- Iterate fast.
- Have a clear vision.
- Remove complexity but keep depth.
- Embrace the medium.
- Use familiar terms.
- Support player stories with randomness.
- Reward with frequent “little victories”.

### From Monster Train / Great Deckbuilders
- Favor **soft synergies** and card combos.
- Rarity tiers should reflect **build tightness** (common = flex, rare = strategy).
- Deckbuilding is about **identity** and **progression**.

### From Persona, SMT, Bravely Default, etc.
- Use **Turn Economy**: limit enemy actions, reward planning.
- Reduce wait times with stylish action feedback.
- Combat menus should be fast, themed, and responsive.

### From Final Fantasy, Pokémon, etc.
- Give players a reason to **always improve**.
- Extrinsic and intrinsic rewards must coexist.
- Skill trees, evolving cards, perks, and NG+ systems help maintain investment.
